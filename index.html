When did your interest in IT start?

When I was about growing up, about the age of 8, I was introduced into the world of IT with the Commodore 64 personal computer and a Nintendo entertainment system. I used the Commodore 64 for games and I also learnt how to program the Commodore 64 using Basic. Later on, after transcribing an assembler/compiler from a magazine, I used hexadecimal to code simple games like a Zaxxon clone. Of course, I used magazines to provide the source code. It was this realisation, that showed that the computer could provide a method for humans to create programs to calculate a desired product.

Later on my family bought a Tandy 1000 - an IBM AT clone.The IBM clone was more business focused and had productive applications. (all be it very simple and unstable.) It also had games for entertainment, but it further opened the possibilities and showed the direction that computers were headed:

More powerful, with each generation. More capable - with unlimited possibilities in the future.

Karma™

Overview:

I am creating a platform that will showcase ‘Karma’ ie. The spirit of good will generated by a person's actions.

It will be public facing and available to people who sign up and become members.

This online platform will track people’s activities within this platform.

he activities can include helping people, through an online submission, notice board and acceptance system.

An example of an activity, may be for socialising with the elderly or helping with giving food for the homeless.

It will be flexible so activities can anything else that is suggested by the community, as long as it is safe and morally and ethically acceptable.

The key driver for the platform will be the Karma™ score.

Members are able to show their Karma score and share it within the Karma platform and other social media platforms.

This platform will be online, accessible via computer, tablet, mobile and watch.


Motivation:

I believe there is a need in today’s world to allow good deeds to be recognised.

It should be encouraged, and it should flourish.

I first came up with this idea about 12 years ago. Since then, it’s been put on the back burner. I have however kept an eye for a similar platform and I have not yet seen a system that offers this type of service.

This recent article shares my sentiment. The article shows that it is an issue that is recognised in today’s society.

Young people aren't volunteering as much. (link)

Members of our society that are not as social, be they elderly, or not as mobile, be they disabled, should be able to have an opportunity to have a willing and able person to liven up their world.

And the charities that help feed the homeless, or the less well to do, I believe would gladly accept any help from volunteers.

1 good deed, deserves another.

I think of it as the new gig economy but for those that want more than just money.

They want a fulfilled and happy conscience.


Description:

Karma will be a social platform where everyday individuals can task, rest and play with the knowledge that they are improving not only their lives, but the lives of the people around them and society in general.

Karma allows volunteering, helping, tasking and enjoying the fruits of your activities in safe environment.

The platform will initially be for volunteering with no money changing hands.

Though eventually it will be able to introduce a system where money can change hands, when both parties agree.

The Karma system will be also eventually be designed to allow trading of goods or services.

In all, this platform will be where everything a member does will contribute to their Karma © score.

It will show how trustworthy a person is. How charitable they are, and the good deeds that they do.

It will have a Karma scoring system where members can share, and be proud of their Karma score.

They can share, and be proud of their achievement and have the admiration of their peers.


In summary, it will be an online system where activities and/or tasks are listed. And members can join that activity, or complete the task.

The focus of the tasks will be for charitible and good willed activities.

And emphasis will also be placed on the safety of the system and the members.


Some of the workflow/milestones of the system will be:

  1. Registration of Users
  2. Background check of users.
  3. ID system and security will be a focus. When parties meet, identification will be available with verified name and photos.
  4. If there is any tasks that are around children, a working with children certificate is required.
  5. Karma levels are based on how many times a person has good reviews.
  6. Depending on the activities the User has performed, and the reviews they get, their Karma score is increased.
  7. There are multiple levels of Karma.
  8. There is encouragement to increase Karma as well as diversify activities.
  9. There will be multiple activities that are on offer. For example:
    1. Volunteering with elderly / aged care homes.
      1. This may include socialising.
      2. Helping with food preparation.
      3. Helping them walk around town etc
    2. Cleaning up a house.
  10. Some tasks may have a monetary reward. We will let the members set it up.
  11. Some tasks may be based on bartering system.
  12. Some tasks may require like for like activities.
    1. Member A house sits Member B and in exchange Member B house sits for Member A at a later time.
    2. Or the Member A house sits Member B. Member B house sits Member C. Member C house sits Member A.
  13. All tasks earn Karma points. There will be a weighting system on how many Karma points are earned with each activity. This may change over time.
  14. Everything is tracked and if a Member doesn’t receive a good review, their Karma is reduced.
  15. A verification and appeal system is available.
  16. Accessibility will be available via all modern devices:
    1. Website / Computer
    2. Mobile phone
    3. Tablet App
    4. Smart watch
  17. Karma will engage with organisations such as charities and aged care providers, to form partnerships.
  18. Karma will be prominent in local communities and also national, and internationally.
  19. Local councils, communities, social clubs along with sporting clubs will be engaged.
  20. Task vacancies can be perused and accepted.
  21. All events, profiles and Karma scores can be shared amongst all the social platforms.

    22. Minimum viable product:

    Create a mobile app and backend server system to host the platform.

    The mobile app should be available for both Android and Apple. I believe that both platforms should be available even as a MVP. As I believe not allowing both platforms will be detrimental to the success of the platform in the long run.

    As people may psychologically be turned off if one platform is favoured over another.

    The backend should consist of a cloud provider hosting the application server and database server.

    The cloud platform will allow elasticity and scale quicky and easily to accommodate any growth, and especially suited to spike growths. If the platform is promoted in a high exposure way.

    Partnerships will be invaluable to the platform, the priority will be to work with private aged care facilities as they may not have as strict a framework, to allow volunteers to engage with the elderly.

    Partnerships with charities such as those giving food to the poor would be available too before launch.

    Government relationships can be worked at a later date.

    The first set of members must be nominated by the orgnisations, and church groups and are invitation only.

    Initially background checks are by referral only from an existing member.

    Later on, financial, police checks and working with children checks can be provided by Karma.

    No cash transactions will be available for the MVP. This is to ease any legislative barriers early on.

    Later on, it can be implemented and advertised once the platform becomes viable for the costs to be incurred and legal counsels can be engaged.

    Karma scores will still be an integral part of the platform.

    And early adopters will be highlighted by a token or avatar as a founding member.

    A responsive and interactive website. Written in HTML5 and Javascript.

    We will have laptop computers for all staff including powerful models for programmers and creative oriented laptops for the designers

    We would of course provide mobile phones to staff members, to use as business function and for app specific testing.

    Cloud service providers to host the servers, both application and database.

    This would allow very quick and easy scalability, allowing the service to grow quickly and easily.

    Programming languages for the back end software include C++, Golang, Java, Python and SQL.

    Although open source office applications are avaible, I would still use the MS Office suite for the typical business utilities ie. Excel, Word etc. as most people are comfortable using this software suite.

    Multiple software developers are required to create the front end applications for IOS, Android.

    Developers for the back end - server side and database management.

    We will need server / cloud administrators to manage the infrastructure.
